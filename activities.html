What to do in Stoke Newington

Stoke Newington is a neighbourhood that is happening. It has something for everyone! Are you a sports enthusiast? Are you a family who want a fun day out wiht the kids? Are you just a couple, wanting to find new things to do in London? Or do you just want to go to a coffee shop and get some work done? Stokey provides for all - seek and you will find.

A most splendid stroll in Stoke Newington

If you want to have a splendid stroll across Stoke Newington, we reccomend the following:

A: Arrive at Manor House Station. It is on the Picadilly Line.

B: From the station, walk to the Woodberry Wetlands. The area has recently been renovated. It will seem as if you're in the countryside, with views of church steeples and the nearby castle, however if you're careul, you'll spot some glimpses of Canary Wharf!

C: Either take the route the map reccomends, or feel free to go all around both resevoirs and properly explore the area. Either way, we reccomend you end up by the impressive castle, one of London's hidden gems. Feel free to enter the castle, it's a climbing centre, also there should be a cafe inside!

D: From the castle, walk southwards on Green Lanes, and enter Clissold Park. At this point feel free to explore - walk around the lakes, or continue on our suggested route towards Clissold House.

E: Don't forget to see the deer!

F: Exit Clissold Park and enter Church Street. Officially called Stoke Newington Church Street, it truly is the hub of the area. Filled with independent shops, restaurants, pubs and those oh-so deseriable coffee shops, Church street has it all, including street art by Banksy! When you exit the Park you will immediately spot two Churches oppisite each other, both named St. Mary's; the old one is now used by artists and for event hire, and the new church is, well, still a church!

G: Try to find a bust of Edgar Allen Poe!

H: If you've visited Highgate or Brompton cemetry, why not visit it's less known sibling - Abney Park Cemetry. One of the original Magnificant Seven (seven cemetries built during the Victorian era, including Highgate), Abney Park Cemetry is filled with impressive grave sites and famous people (such as Salvation Army founders William and Mary Booth). It's also where Amy Winhouse shot her video for 'Back to Black'. Unlike Highgate Cemetery, it's free to visit!

Sports activities

If you're a sports enthusiast, then Stokey has a number of facilities. For example, amongst its other collection of animals (though don't confuse it for a zoo; it's only a small collection). Clissold Park is popular amongst North Londoners in general, as it has a skate park, a tennis courts and basketball courts, so more than just your basic jog! Fairs & Festivals

Stoke Newington has two main events not to missed: The Stoke Newington Literary Festival, and the Stoke Newington Music Festival. As the websites for these events are rarely udpated, we reccomend simply Googling them to see if there are any updates on the exact dates. Although technically not Stoke Newington, Wireless Festival is held in Finsbury Park, on the border of Stoke Newington. It's a rap and urban music festival held in the first - notable celebrities feature every year. Though not a fair nor festival, Hackney Carnival usually takes place around the summer time every year, and sometimes is hosted in Ridley Road Market, in nearby Dalston.

Beyond Stoke Newington

Stoke Newington neighbors a number of diverse and happening areas. If you're on Stoke Newington high street, at one end you'll have Dalston, the hyper-gentrified neighborhood is filled with bars, pubs, clubs, restaurants, shops and whatever ones heart could desire. On the other side you have Stamford Hill, the biggest Orthodox Jewish neighborhood in Europe. If you're around Clissold Park, a stroll towards Canonbury or Highbury and Islington will take you through leafy neighbourhoods surrounded by delightful architecture. If you're around Woodberry Wetlands, you'll be near by Finsbury Park, which on the other end has another hidden gem: the Parkland Walk, which allows you to stroll on a disused railroad all the way to Highgate and up to Alexander Palace. If you want to stay closer by, Harringay Green Lanes is another one of those gentrified areas, famous for its countless Turkish/Kurdish restaurants.
