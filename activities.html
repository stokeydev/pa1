A most splendid stroll in Stoke Newington

If you come to Stoke Newington via Manor House, you won't miss the the Caslte. Built as a pumping station in the 1850s. It's use became redunant by the 1970s and local campaigners saved it from redevelopers attempts to tear it down for housing. It eventually became a climbing centre, and has become world-renowend by climbing enthusiasts. It's right by the two resevoirs which has seen a lot of improvement in the last decade, with David Attenborough reopening the site after the Wild Life Trust's work on improving the site, and saw Prince William recently visit and having a bit of a row while filiming for the program 'Prince William: A Planet For Us All' in 2020.

Clissold Park

You don't need to go to Richmond to see deer in London - Clissold Park is home to our four-legged friends, amongst its other collection of animals (though don't confuse it for a zoo; it's only a small collection). Clissold Park is popular amongst North Londoners in general, as it has a skate park, a tennis courts and basketball courts.

Abney Park Cemetery

If you've visited Highgate or Brompton cemetry, why not visit it's less known sibling - Abney Park Cemetry. One of the original Magnificant Seven (seven cemetries built during the Victorian era, including Highgate), Abney Park Cemetry is filled with impressive grave sites and famous people (such as Salvation Army founders William and Mary Booth). It's also where Amy Winhouse shot her video for 'Back to Black'. Unlike Highgate Cemetery, it's free to visit!

Church Street

Officially called Stoke Newington Church Street, it truly is the hub of the area. Filled with independent shops, restaurants, pubs and those oh-so deseriable coffee shops, Church street has it all, including street art by Banksy!
