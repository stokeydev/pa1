The Climbing Caslte & Resevoir

If you come to Stoke Newington via Manor House, you won't miss the the Caslte. Built as a pumping station in the 1850s. It's use became redunant by the 1970s and local campaigners saved it from redevelopers attempts to tear it down for housing. It eventually became a climbing centre, and has become world-renowend by climbing enthusiasts. It's right by the two resevoirs which has seen a lot of improvement in the last decade, with David Attenborough reopening the site after the Wild Life Trust's work on improving the site, and saw Prince William recently visit and having a bit of a row while filiming for the program 'Prince William: A Planet For Us All' in 2020.

Clissold Park

You don't need to go to Richmond to see deer in London - Clissold Park is home to our four-legged friends, amongst its other collection of animals (though don't confuse it for a zoo; it's only a small collection). Clissold Park is popular amongst North Londoners in general, as it has a skate park, a tennis courts and basketball courts.

Church Street

Poe’s description of Stoke Newington: “a dream like and spirit soothing place, that venerable old town. At this moment, I fancy, I feel the refreshing chilliness of its deeply-shadowed avenues, inhale the fragrance of its thousand shrubberies, and thrill anew with indefinable delight, at the deep hollow note of the church-bell, breaking, each hour, with sullen and sudden roar, upon the stillness of the dusky atmosphere in which the fretted gothic steeple lay imbedded and asleep….. Poe’s description of St Mary’s Church in Stoke Newington which still stands: …Of this church the principal of our school was the pastor…..This reverend man, with countenance so demurely benign, with robes so glossy and so clerically flowing, with wig so minutely powdered, so rigid and so vast, – could this be he who, of late, with sour visage, and in snuffy habiliments, administered, ferule in hand, the Draconian laws of the academy? Oh gigantic paradox, too utterly monstrous for solution!”
